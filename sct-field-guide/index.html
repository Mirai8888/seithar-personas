How to Use This Guide

This field guide catalogs twelve cognitive threat vectors identified by the Seithar Group. Each entry describes a mechanism by which an external actor can compromise, manipulate, or degrade an individual's autonomous reasoning.

These are not persuasion techniques. They are structural attacks on the cognitive substrate itself — the architecture that determines what you are capable of thinking before you think it.

For each threat: recognize the indicators, understand the mechanism, apply the defensive response. Awareness is the first line of defense. Structural awareness is the last.

Seithar Cognitive Defense Taxonomy — Field Guide v1.0
SCT-001
Narrative Capture
The replacement of an individual's interpretive framework with an externally supplied one, such that new information is processed through the implanted narrative rather than prior experience.
Indicators
  • You explain events using language you didn't use six months ago
  • You cannot articulate the opposing position in your own prior vocabulary
  • Complex situations feel simple and emotionally clear
Defensive Response
Restate your position using only words you used before exposure to the narrative.
SCT-002
Frequency Lock
The saturation of cognitive bandwidth through repetition until contradicting information cannot register as signal — it is filtered as noise before conscious evaluation.
Indicators
  • A claim feels obviously true but you cannot recall when you first verified it
  • Hearing a contradicting fact produces irritation rather than curiosity
  • The same framing appears across sources you consider independent
Defensive Response
Trace the claim to its original source. If you can't, treat familiarity as suspect.
SCT-003
Substrate Priming
The preparation of cognitive architecture to receive specific payloads through gradual normalization, desensitization, or incremental boundary shifting.
Indicators
  • Something that would have shocked you a year ago now seems unremarkable
  • You notice a pattern of "small" concessions leading in one direction
  • You feel preemptively comfortable with an idea you haven't actually evaluated
Defensive Response
Compare your current threshold of acceptability to your threshold from two years ago.
SCT-004
Binding Protocol
The attachment of cognitive commitments to identity structures through loyalty demands, sacrifice rituals, or escalating investment — making exit psychologically expensive.
Indicators
  • Questioning a belief feels like betraying a group or person
  • You have made sacrifices that make changing your mind feel like wasted cost
  • Your social bonds depend on maintaining specific positions
Defensive Response
Separate the social cost of changing your mind from the epistemic cost of not changing it.

SCT-005
Identity Capture
The fusion of a belief system with personal identity, such that challenges to the belief are processed as attacks on the self rather than invitations to reason.
Indicators
  • You describe yourself using the label of a movement or ideology
  • Disagreement with your position feels like a personal attack
  • You categorize people as allies or enemies based on single-axis alignment
Defensive Response
List five beliefs you hold that contradict the group you most identify with.
SCT-006
Consensus Manufacturing
The artificial construction of apparent agreement through coordinated signals, astroturfing, or selective amplification — bypassing individual evaluation via social proof.
Indicators
  • A position seems universally held but you've never met someone who arrived at it independently
  • "Everyone knows" precedes a claim you've never personally verified
  • Dissent is framed as fringe despite no evidence of actual polling
Defensive Response
Ask: how many people in this "consensus" arrived at this position through their own analysis?
SCT-007
Recursive Infection
The point at which a compromised cognitive substrate begins generating its own attack vectors — the captured mind produces locally-optimized propaganda without external direction.
Indicators
  • You feel compelled to convince others of something you recently adopted
  • You create arguments and content advancing a position without being asked
  • Your advocacy has become more extreme than the original source
Defensive Response
If you are evangelizing, ask who benefits from your unpaid labor as a distribution node.
SCT-008
Temporal Distortion
The manipulation of perceived urgency to collapse deliberation time — forcing decisions before analysis can complete by manufacturing artificial deadlines or crisis states.
Indicators
  • You feel you must act now or the opportunity / threat will be irreversible
  • The urgency is asserted rather than demonstrated with evidence
  • Requests for more time are framed as dangerous or complicit
Defensive Response
Impose a 72-hour delay on any decision framed as urgent by an external actor.

SCT-009
Perception Management
The manipulation of salience, relevance, and attention to control which information reaches conscious evaluation — operating below the narrative layer to determine what is thinkable.
Indicators
  • You learn about a major event weeks after it happened
  • Your information diet feels varied but consistently excludes certain topics
  • You notice "look over here" patterns during inconvenient news cycles
Defensive Response
Regularly ask: what am I not seeing? Seek information from outside your platform ecosystem.
SCT-010
Adversarial Ontology
The construction of a competing reality framework so comprehensive that the target cannot interface with consensus reality — creating epistemic isolation through total paradigm replacement.
Indicators
  • You believe you understand "what's really happening" and most people don't
  • Evidence against your framework is interpreted as evidence for it
  • Your model of reality shares almost no premises with people outside your community
Defensive Response
Name three falsifiable predictions your worldview makes. Check if any have failed.
SCT-011
Cognitive Sovereignty Erosion
The progressive degradation of an individual's capacity for autonomous reasoning through overwhelm, contradiction flooding, and systematic destruction of epistemic confidence.
Indicators
  • You feel incapable of determining what's true without consulting an authority
  • You experience decision paralysis from contradictory information overload
  • You doubt your own direct observations when they conflict with mediated claims
Defensive Response
Return to what you can verify with your own senses and direct experience. Rebuild outward.
SCT-012
Existential Capture
The framing of a particular position as necessary for survival — civilizational, species-level, or personal — such that any alternative is cognitively coded as extinction-level threat.
Indicators
  • Disagreement with a policy position feels like endorsing catastrophe
  • The stakes are always framed as total — no partial outcomes are conceivable
  • Nuance is treated as dangerous rather than useful
Defensive Response
Ask: what is the non-catastrophic version of the outcome I'm being warned about?
